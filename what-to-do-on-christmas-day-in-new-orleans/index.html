What To Do On Christmas Day In New Orleans

Written by Bowie Mar 09, 2023 · 3 min read

.

New Orleans is a great place to spend Christmas day. There are plenty of things to do that are fun, festive, and family-friendly. In this article, we’ll explore the best ways to spend Christmas day in New Orleans. Here’s what you can expect:

Table of Contents

  • Explore the French Quarter
  • Visit Jackson Square
  • Enjoy a Christmas Meal at a Local Restaurant
  • Take a Stroll through City Park
  • Go on a Festive Carriage Ride
  • Take a Riverboat Cruise
  • FAQs

Explore the French Quarter

The French Quarter is the heart of New Orleans, and it’s especially charming during the holidays. The streets are decked out in lights and wreaths, and there’s plenty of live music and entertainment to enjoy. You can take a leisurely stroll down Bourbon Street, stop by one of the many art galleries, or visit a historic site like the St. Louis Cathedral.

Visit Jackson Square

Jackson Square is another must-visit destination in New Orleans. During the holidays, the square is transformed into a winter wonderland with festive decorations and music. You can take a picture with the iconic statue of Andrew Jackson, listen to live jazz, or watch street performers.

Enjoy a Christmas Meal at a Local Restaurant

New Orleans is known for its delicious cuisine, and Christmas day is no exception. Many local restaurants offer special holiday menus with traditional dishes like gumbo, jambalaya, and crawfish étouffée. Some popular restaurants include Commander’s Palace, Antoine’s, and Galatoire’s.

Take a Stroll through City Park

City Park is a beautiful oasis in the heart of New Orleans, and it’s especially magical during the holidays. The park is home to one of the largest oak groves in the world, as well as several lagoons and gardens. During the holidays, the park is decorated with twinkling lights and festive displays.

Go on a Festive Carriage Ride

A carriage ride through the French Quarter is a charming way to see the sights and sounds of New Orleans during the holidays. Many carriage companies offer special Christmas tours that take you through the most festive parts of the city. You can cuddle up with a loved one and enjoy the sights and sounds of the season.

Take a Riverboat Cruise

A riverboat cruise down the Mississippi River is a unique way to spend Christmas day in New Orleans. Many riverboat companies offer special holiday cruises that include live music, food, and drinks. You can take in the sights and sounds of the city from a whole new perspective.

FAQs

What is the weather like in New Orleans on Christmas day?

The weather in New Orleans during the holidays is usually mild, with temperatures ranging from the 50s to 60s.

What are some other festive things to do in New Orleans during the holidays?

Other festive things to do in New Orleans during the holidays include visiting the Christmas lights at Lafreniere Park, attending a holiday concert at the Orpheum Theater, and taking a tour of the historic homes in the Garden District.

Where can I find more information about Christmas events in New Orleans?

You can find more information about Christmas events in New Orleans by visiting the official tourism website, NewOrleans.com, or by checking local event calendars like the Gambit Weekly. In conclusion, spending Christmas day in New Orleans is a unique and festive experience. Whether you’re exploring the French Quarter, enjoying a delicious meal, or taking a carriage ride, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So, pack your bags and head to the Big Easy for a holiday you won’t forget!
