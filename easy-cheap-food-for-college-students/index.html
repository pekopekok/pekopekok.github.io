Easy Cheap Food For College Students A Guide

Written by Jordan Mar 03, 2023 · 4 min read

As a college student it s understandable that you re constantly on a tight budget Eating out can be expensive and cooking can be time consuming However it s important to eat healthy and nutritious food to keep up with the demands of studying and attending classes In this article we ll provide you with a guide on easy cheap food for college students that won t break the bank .

As a college student, it’s understandable that you’re constantly on a tight budget. Eating out can be expensive and cooking can be time-consuming. However, it’s important to eat healthy and nutritious food to keep up with the demands of studying and attending classes. In this article, we’ll provide you with a guide on easy cheap food for college students that won’t break the bank.

Ramen

Ramen is a classic staple for college students. It’s cheap, easy to make, and can be customized to your liking. You can add vegetables, protein, and even a boiled egg to make it more filling and nutritious. Just be mindful of the sodium content and try to opt for low-sodium options or add less seasoning.

PB&J

Peanut butter and jelly sandwiches are another easy and cheap option. They’re perfect for a quick snack or lunch in between classes. You can also switch it up by using almond or cashew butter and different types of jelly or jam.

Eggs

Eggs are a versatile and nutritious option for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. You can make scrambled eggs, omelets, or even a breakfast sandwich. Eggs are also a good source of protein and can help keep you full throughout the day.

Beans

Beans are a great source of protein and fiber. They’re also cheap and can be used in a variety of dishes such as chili, tacos, and salads. You can even make your own hummus with chickpeas or black beans.

Oatmeal

Oatmeal is a healthy and filling option for breakfast. You can add fruit, nuts, or honey to sweeten it up. Oatmeal is also a good source of fiber and can help keep you full until your next meal.

Soup

Soup is a great way to use up leftover vegetables and protein. You can make a big batch and freeze it for later. Soup is also a comforting and warming option during the colder months.

Stir Fry

Stir fry is a quick and easy option for dinner. You can use any vegetables and protein you have on hand and season it with soy sauce or other spices. You can also serve it with rice or noodles for a more filling meal.

Quesadilla

Quesadillas are a delicious and easy option for lunch or dinner. You can use any type of cheese and add vegetables or protein. You can also serve it with salsa, guacamole, or sour cream for added flavor.

Pasta

Pasta is a classic and filling option for dinner. You can make a simple tomato sauce or add vegetables and protein. Pasta is also a great option for meal prep and can be eaten throughout the week.

Smoothie

Smoothies are a nutritious and refreshing option for breakfast or a snack. You can use any type of fruit and add yogurt or protein powder for extra protein. Smoothies are also a great way to use up fruit that’s about to go bad.

Tips for Eating Cheaply in College

Here are some tips to help you eat cheaply in college:

  • Meal prep on the weekends to save time and money during the week
  • Buy in bulk to save money
  • Use coupons and discounts when shopping
  • Shop at discount grocery stores
  • Avoid eating out too often

Pros and Cons of Eating Cheaply in College

Here are some pros and cons of eating cheaply in college:

Pros

  • Save money
  • Eat healthier
  • Learn how to cook

Cons

  • Limited options
  • Can be time-consuming
  • May not be as convenient as eating out

FAQs

What are some other cheap and easy options for college students?

Other options include mac and cheese, grilled cheese, and baked potatoes.

Is it possible to eat healthy on a budget?

Yes, it’s possible to eat healthy on a budget. Look for sales on produce and opt for frozen fruits and vegetables when fresh isn’t available.

How can I make cooking more fun?

Listen to music or a podcast while cooking and try new recipes.

We hope this guide on easy cheap food for college students is helpful. Remember to prioritize your health and budget when making food choices. Happy cooking!
