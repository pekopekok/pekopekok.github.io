How to Split for Beginners - Tips, Step by Step Guide, Pros and Cons (2023)

If you're new to the world of fitness or simply looking to switch up your routine, splitting your workouts may be the way to go. Splitting refers to dividing your workout routine into specific muscle groups on different days, allowing for more targeted training and quicker recovery times. In this guide, we'll cover the basics of how to split for beginners with step by step instructions, tips, and pros and cons.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine your goals
  • Step 2: Choose a split routine
  • Step 3: Plan your workouts
  • Step 4: Start slow and adjust as needed
  • Tips for Success

Step 1: Determine your goals

Before diving into a split routine, it's important to determine your fitness goals. Are you looking to build strength, increase muscle mass, or improve overall fitness? Depending on your goals, you may choose a different split routine. For example, if your goal is to build strength, a push-pull-legs split may be best.

Step 2: Choose a split routine

Once you've determined your goals, it's time to choose a split routine. There are several popular split routines, including:

  • Upper/Lower Split
  • Push/Pull/Legs Split
  • Full Body Split
  • Body Part Split

Research each routine and choose one that aligns with your goals and fits your schedule.

Step 3: Plan your workouts

After choosing a split routine, it's time to plan your workouts. Determine which exercises you'll perform on each day and how many sets and reps you'll do. Be sure to include a warm-up and cool-down in each workout.

Step 4: Start slow and adjust as needed

It's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts as your body adapts. Listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed. If you experience pain or discomfort, take a break and consult with a medical professional.

Tips for Success

  • Stay hydrated throughout your workouts
  • Include rest days in your routine for proper recovery
  • Vary your exercises to prevent boredom and plateauing
  • Track your progress to stay motivated

FAQs

Q: How often should I work out?

A: This depends on your fitness goals and schedule. As a beginner, it's recommended to start with 3-4 days per week and gradually increase as your body adapts.

Q: Is splitting better than full body workouts?

A: This depends on your goals and preferences. Splitting allows for more targeted training, but full body workouts can be effective for overall fitness and time efficiency.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Allows for targeted training
  • Quicker recovery times
  • Variety in workouts

Cons:

  • May require more time and planning
  • May not be suitable for all fitness goals
  • May increase risk of injury if not done properly

With these tips and step by step instructions, you can confidently start splitting your workouts for more targeted training and quicker recovery times. Remember to start slow, listen to your body, and adjust your routine as needed.
