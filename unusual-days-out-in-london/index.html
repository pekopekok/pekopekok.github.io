From exploring abandoned tube stations to visiting the world's oldest toy store, there's something for everyone. So, let's dive in!

How to Explore the Abandoned Tube Stations

London's transport system is one of the most extensive in the world, with over 270 stations. However, did you know that there are several abandoned stations that are no longer in use? These stations are fascinating to explore, and they offer a glimpse into London's transport history.

The best way to explore these stations is by going on a tour. Several companies offer guided tours of these stations, including Hidden London and London Transport Museum. These tours will take you through the abandoned stations, including the tunnels, platforms, and control rooms. You'll also learn about the history of the stations and the reasons why they were closed.

Some of the most popular abandoned stations to explore are Aldwych, Down Street, and Brompton Road. These stations have been closed for decades and offer a unique insight into the past.

Step by Step Guide to Visiting the World's Oldest Toy Store

If you're a fan of toys, then a visit to the world's oldest toy store, Hamleys, is a must. This iconic store has been around since 1760 and is a paradise for kids and adults alike. Here's a step-by-step guide to visiting Hamleys:

Step 1: Plan Your Visit

Hamleys is located on Regent Street in the heart of London. You can get there by taking the tube to Oxford Circus station. The store is open every day, so plan your visit accordingly.

Step 2: Explore the Store

Once you arrive at Hamleys, take your time to explore the store. There are seven floors of toys to discover, including board games, dolls, action figures, and much more. Make sure to visit the famous Hamleys teddy bear section, which is a favourite among visitors.

Step 3: Attend a Toy Demonstration

One of the highlights of visiting Hamleys is watching the toy demonstrations. The store has dedicated areas where staff members showcase the latest toys and gadgets. Make sure to attend one of these demonstrations for a unique experience.

Step 4: Shop for Souvenirs

No visit to Hamleys is complete without buying a souvenir. The store has a wide range of toys and merchandise to choose from, including exclusive Hamleys teddy bears and t-shirts.

Tips for Visiting the Old Operating Theatre Museum

The Old Operating Theatre Museum is a hidden gem in London that's often overlooked by tourists. This museum is located in the attic of St Thomas' Church and is the oldest surviving operating theatre in Europe. Here are some tips for visiting the museum:

Tip 1: Book in Advance

The museum is small, and tickets are limited, so it's essential to book in advance. You can book tickets online or by phone.

Tip 2: Wear Comfortable Shoes

The museum is located in the attic of St Thomas' Church and requires climbing several flights of stairs. Wear comfortable shoes to make the climb more comfortable.

Tip 3: Attend a Talk or Tour

The museum offers talks and tours that provide a deeper insight into the history of the operating theatre. Attend one of these for a more immersive experience.

FAQ About Unusual Days Out in London

What are some other unusual days out in London?

Other unusual days out in London include visiting the Hunterian Museum, exploring the abandoned Highgate Cemetery, and taking a street art tour of East London.

Are these unusual days out suitable for children?

It depends on the activity. Some activities, such as visiting Hamleys, are suitable for children, while others, such as exploring abandoned tube stations, may not be suitable for young children.

Pros and Cons of Unusual Days Out in London

Cons:

Overall, unusual days out in London are a fantastic way to explore the city and discover hidden gems. Whether you're interested in history, art, or toys, there's something for everyone. So, the next time you're in London, why not try something different and explore the unique side of the city?
