What is Push Pull Legs Split?

Push pull legs split is a workout routine that focuses on three major muscle groups: push muscles (chest, shoulders, and triceps), pull muscles (back and biceps), and leg muscles (quads, hamstrings, and calves). The idea behind this split is to work each muscle group twice a week, with a day of rest in between.

The push pull legs split routine is popular among bodybuilders and fitness enthusiasts because it allows for a balanced workout that targets all major muscle groups while promoting fat loss. By alternating between push, pull, and leg exercises, you can keep your heart rate up and burn calories while building muscle.

How to Do Push Pull Legs Split

Before you start the push pull legs split routine, it's important to make sure you have a good understanding of the exercises you'll be doing. This routine involves compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once, so it's essential to use proper form to avoid injury and get the most out of your workouts.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to the push pull legs split routine:

  • Push day: On push day, focus on exercises that target your chest, shoulders, and triceps. Examples of push exercises include bench press, shoulder press, and tricep dips.
  • Pull day: On pull day, focus on exercises that target your back and biceps. Examples of pull exercises include pull-ups, rows, and bicep curls.
  • Leg day: On leg day, focus on exercises that target your quads, hamstrings, and calves. Examples of leg exercises include squats, lunges, and calf raises.
  • Rest day: Take a rest day between each workout to allow your muscles to recover and prevent injury.
  • Repeat: Repeat the push pull legs split routine twice a week, alternating between push, pull, and leg days.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips for getting the most out of your push pull legs split routine:

  • Use proper form: Make sure you're using proper form for each exercise to avoid injury and get the most benefit from your workouts.
  • Progressive overload: Increase the weight or reps of each exercise over time to continue challenging your muscles and promoting growth.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workouts to stay hydrated and prevent cramping.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, complex carbs, and healthy fats can help fuel your workouts and promote muscle growth.
  • Get enough rest: Getting enough rest and sleep is essential for muscle recovery and growth.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the push pull legs split routine:

  • Can I do this routine if I'm a beginner? Yes, but it's important to start with light weights and focus on proper form to avoid injury.
  • How long should each workout last? Aim for 45-60 minutes per workout.
  • How often should I do this routine? Twice a week, with a day of rest in between each workout.
  • Can I combine push, pull, and leg exercises in one workout? Yes, but it's important to focus on one muscle group at a time to avoid overtraining.

Pros and Cons

Here are some pros and cons of the push pull legs split routine:

  • Pros: Targets all major muscle groups, promotes fat loss, and can be customized to fit your fitness goals.
  • Cons: Can be time-consuming, may not be suitable for beginners, and requires access to gym equipment.

Overall, the push pull legs split routine is a great way to promote weight loss, build muscle, and improve your overall fitness. By following the tips and guidelines outlined in this article, you can get the most out of your workouts and achieve your fitness goals.
