What is Fatty Liver Disease?

Fatty liver disease is a condition in which excess fat builds up in the liver. This can cause inflammation and damage to the liver, which can lead to serious health problems such as cirrhosis and liver cancer. There are two types of fatty liver disease: alcoholic fatty liver disease and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD). Alcoholic fatty liver disease is caused by excessive alcohol consumption, while NAFLD is caused by factors such as obesity, insulin resistance, and high cholesterol.

Tips for Following the 7 Day Meal Plan for Fatty Liver Indian

Here are some tips that can help you follow the 7 day meal plan for fatty liver Indian:

  • Choose whole grains instead of refined grains.
  • Include plenty of fruits and vegetables in your diet.
  • Choose lean protein sources such as chicken, fish, and tofu.
  • Avoid processed foods and sugary drinks.
  • Limit your intake of saturated and trans fats.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.

FAQs

What foods should I avoid if I have fatty liver disease?

If you have fatty liver disease, you should avoid foods that are high in saturated and trans fats, added sugars, and refined grains. Examples include fried foods, processed snacks, sugary drinks, and white bread.

Can I drink alcohol if I have fatty liver disease?

If you have fatty liver disease, it is best to avoid alcohol altogether. Alcohol can worsen liver damage and increase the risk of liver cancer.
