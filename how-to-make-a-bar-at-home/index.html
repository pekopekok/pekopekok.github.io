How To Make A Bar At Home A Complete Guide

Written by Jordan Mar 27, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking to create a fun and entertaining space in your home, why not consider setting up your own bar? Not only will it be a great place to entertain guests, but it can also be a relaxing space for you to unwind after a long day. In this guide, we'll take you through how to make a bar at home step by step, including tips on design, equipment, and drinks.

How to Set Up Your Home Bar

Before you start buying equipment and stocking up on alcohol, you need to decide where to put your home bar. Ideally, you should choose a space that's out of the way but still easily accessible. It could be a corner of your living room, a spare bedroom, or even your garage if you have the space. Once you've chosen your location, you can start thinking about the design of your bar.

Designing Your Home Bar

The design of your bar will depend on your personal style and the space you have available. You can either build a bar from scratch or buy a pre-made one. If you're handy with tools, building your own bar can be a fun DIY project. However, if you're not confident in your DIY skills, buying a pre-made bar can be a good option. Just make sure it fits in with the rest of your home décor.

Equipment and Supplies

Once you have your bar set up, you'll need to stock it with the right equipment and supplies. The basics you'll need include:

  • Shaker
  • Jigger
  • Strainer
  • Muddler
  • Corkscrew
  • Ice bucket and tongs
  • Glassware (highball, wine, martini, shot glasses, etc.)
  • Bar spoon
  • Cutting board and knife

You'll also need to stock up on alcohol, mixers, and other supplies like napkins, coasters, and stirrers. Start with the basics like vodka, gin, rum, whiskey, and tequila, and then add other spirits and mixers as you need them.

Steps to Make a Bar at Home

Step 1: Choose Your Location

As we mentioned earlier, you need to choose a location for your home bar. Make sure it's a space that's out of the way but still easily accessible. You don't want your bar to take up too much room or be in the way of other activities.

Step 2: Decide on the Design

Design your bar to match your personal style and the rest of your home décor. You can either build a bar from scratch or buy a pre-made one. Make sure it has enough storage space for all your equipment and supplies.

Step 3: Stock Up on Equipment and Supplies

Make a list of all the equipment and supplies you'll need for your home bar. Start with the basics and add more as you need them. Make sure you have enough storage space to keep everything organized.

Step 4: Stock Up on Alcohol and Mixers

Start with the basics like vodka, gin, rum, whiskey, and tequila. Then, add other spirits and mixers as you need them. Make sure you have enough of each to last for several gatherings.

Step 5: Organize Your Equipment and Supplies

Organize your equipment and supplies in a way that makes sense to you. Keep your most frequently used equipment and supplies within easy reach. Use storage containers and shelves to keep everything organized.

Step 6: Invite Some Friends Over

Now that you have your home bar set up, invite some friends over to enjoy it with you! Show off your mixology skills and have some fun experimenting with different drinks.

Tips for Making a Bar at Home

  • Choose a location that's out of the way but still easily accessible.
  • Design your bar to match your personal style and the rest of your home décor.
  • Stock up on the basic equipment and supplies you'll need.
  • Start with the basics when it comes to alcohol and mixers.
  • Organize your equipment and supplies in a way that makes sense to you.
  • Invite some friends over to enjoy your new home bar!

FAQ

What kind of alcohol should I stock up on?

Start with the basics like vodka, gin, rum, whiskey, and tequila. Then, add other spirits and mixers as you need them. Make sure you have enough of each to last for several gatherings.

Do I need to build my own bar or can I buy one?

You can either build a bar from scratch or buy a pre-made one. It depends on your DIY skills and personal preference.

What equipment do I need for my home bar?

You'll need a shaker, jigger, strainer, muddler, corkscrew, ice bucket and tongs, glassware (highball, wine, martini, shot glasses, etc.), bar spoon, and cutting board and knife. You'll also need other supplies like napkins, coasters, and stirrers.

Pros and Cons of Making a Bar at Home

Pros:

  • It's a fun and entertaining space for you and your guests.
  • You can experiment with different drinks and mixology skills.
  • You can save money by making your own drinks instead of going out.

Cons:

  • It can be expensive to set up a home bar.
  • You need a dedicated space for your home bar.
  • You need to stock up on equipment and supplies.

In conclusion, setting up a home bar can be a fun and entertaining project that can also save you money in the long run. By following our guide on how to make a bar at home, you'll be well on your way to creating a space that's perfect for entertaining guests or just relaxing with a drink after a long day. Just remember to stock up on the basics, organize your supplies, and invite some friends over to enjoy your new bar!
