The British Museum

The British Museum is one of the most popular free attractions in London. It's home to a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits from all over the world. The museum has over 8 million objects, including the Rosetta Stone and the Elgin Marbles. There's no admission fee, but donations are encouraged.

How to Get There

The British Museum is located in Bloomsbury, in the heart of London. The nearest tube stations are Holborn and Tottenham Court Road.

Tips

  • Plan your visit in advance and decide which exhibits you want to see.
  • Arrive early to avoid the crowds.
  • Check the museum's website for special events and exhibitions.

The Tate Modern

The Tate Modern is one of the world's most famous modern art museums. It's located in an old power station on the banks of the River Thames. The museum has a vast collection of contemporary art, including works by Picasso, Warhol, and Hockney. Admission is free, but some special exhibitions may have a fee.

How to Get There

The Tate Modern is located on Bankside, near Blackfriars Bridge. The nearest tube stations are Southwark and Blackfriars.

Tips

  • Visit the museum's website for information on current exhibitions.
  • Take a free guided tour of the museum.
  • Enjoy the view from the museum's terrace.

The National Gallery

The National Gallery is home to one of the world's greatest collections of Western European paintings. The museum has over 2,300 works of art, including masterpieces by Van Gogh, Leonardo da Vinci, and Rembrandt. Admission is free, but some special exhibitions may have a fee.

How to Get There

The National Gallery is located in Trafalgar Square, in the heart of London. The nearest tube stations are Charing Cross and Leicester Square.

Tips

  • Plan your visit in advance and decide which paintings you want to see.
  • Take a free guided tour of the museum.
  • Visit the museum's website for information on special events and exhibitions.

The Victoria and Albert Museum

The Victoria and Albert Museum is the world's largest museum of decorative arts and design. The museum has over 2.3 million objects, including ceramics, furniture, textiles, and fashion. Admission is free, but some special exhibitions may have a fee.

How to Get There

The Victoria and Albert Museum is located in South Kensington, near the Natural History Museum and the Science Museum. The nearest tube station is South Kensington.

Tips

  • Visit the museum's website for information on current exhibitions.
  • Take a free guided tour of the museum.
  • Visit the museum's gift shop for unique souvenirs.

The Science Museum

The Science Museum is one of the most popular free attractions in London. It's home to a vast collection of scientific exhibits, including the Apollo 10 command module and a full-size model of the DNA molecule. Admission is free, but some special exhibitions may have a fee.

How to Get There

The Changing of the Guard

The Changing of the Guard is a popular ceremony that takes place outside Buckingham Palace. It's a colorful spectacle that features the Queen's Guard, dressed in their traditional red tunics and bearskin hats. The ceremony takes place daily at 11:00 AM during the summer months and every other day during the winter months. Admission is free.

How to Get There

Buckingham Palace is located in Westminster, in the heart of London. The nearest tube stations are Victoria and Green Park.

Tips

  • Arrive early to get a good view of the ceremony.
  • Don't stand too close to the guards or try to touch their hats.
  • Visit the palace's website for information on tours and special events.

The Ceremony of the Keys

The Ceremony of the Keys is a tradition that takes place every night at the Tower of London. It's a 700-year-old ceremony that involves locking up the Tower for the night. Visitors can watch the ceremony for free, but tickets must be booked in advance.

How to Get There

The Tower of London is located in the City of London, near Tower Bridge. The nearest tube station is Tower Hill.

Tips

  • Book your tickets in advance to avoid disappointment.
  • Arrive early to explore the Tower of London before the ceremony.
  • Visit the Tower of London's website for information on tours and special events.

The Sky Garden

The Sky Garden is a public garden located on the 35th floor of the Walkie Talkie building in the City of London. It offers stunning views of the city skyline and is a great place to relax and enjoy a drink. Admission is free, but tickets must be booked in advance.

How to Get There

The Walkie Talkie building is located in the City of London, near Monument. The nearest tube station is Monument.

Tips

  • Book your tickets in advance to avoid disappointment.
  • Visit the Sky Garden's website for information on special events and exhibitions.
  • Enjoy a drink at the Sky Pod Bar.
