High Tea Definition And Examples A Guide To This British Tradition

.

If you're looking for a quintessentially British experience, high tea is a must-try. This tradition has been around for centuries and is still popular today. In this article, we'll explore the high tea definition and examples, how to experience high tea, and some tips to make the most of this experience.

What is High Tea?

High tea, also known as afternoon tea, is a British tradition that dates back to the 1800s. It was originally a meal for the working class, served around 6 pm after a long day's work. The meal consisted of tea, bread, butter, and sometimes cheese or meat. However, in the late 1800s, the upper class adopted this meal as a social event, and it became a more elaborate affair.

Examples of High Tea

Today, high tea is a luxurious experience that typically includes a selection of sandwiches, scones with clotted cream and jam, cakes, and of course, tea. Some examples of high tea items include:
  • Finger sandwiches (e.g. cucumber and cream cheese, smoked salmon and cream cheese, egg mayonnaise)
  • Scones with clotted cream and jam
  • Mini quiches or pies
  • Assorted cakes and pastries
  • Tea (black, green, or herbal)

How to Experience High Tea

If you're interested in experiencing high tea, there are a few options available. You can book a reservation at a hotel or restaurant that offers high tea, or you can host your own at home. If you choose to host your own, be sure to have all the necessary items, including a tea set, tea cups, saucers, and plates.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hosting Your Own High Tea

  1. Choose your menu and gather ingredients
  2. Set the table with a tablecloth, tea set, and plates
  3. Prepare the tea and place it in a teapot
  4. Serve sandwiches and other savory items on tiered stands
  5. Bring out the scones with clotted cream and jam
  6. Finish with cakes and pastries
  7. Enjoy!

Tips for a Successful High Tea Experience

To make the most of your high tea experience, consider the following tips:
  • Dress appropriately - high tea is typically a dressy occasion
  • Arrive on time - high tea is often served at a specific time
  • Practice good etiquette - use your utensils correctly and avoid talking with your mouth full
  • Try everything - high tea is meant to be a sampling of many different items
  • Take your time - high tea is a leisurely affair, so don't rush

FAQ

What is the difference between high tea and afternoon tea?

High tea and afternoon tea are often used interchangeably, but they originally referred to different meals. Afternoon tea was a light meal served in the afternoon, while high tea was a more substantial meal served in the early evening.

What should I wear to high tea?

High tea is a dressy occasion, so it's best to wear smart casual or semi-formal attire. Men should wear trousers and a collared shirt, while women can wear a dress or skirt with a blouse.

Do I need to make a reservation for high tea?

It's always best to make a reservation for high tea, especially if you're going to a popular venue. This will ensure that you have a table and that the venue can accommodate any dietary restrictions.

Pros and Cons of High Tea

Pros:

  • High tea is a fun and unique experience
  • You get to try a variety of different foods and teas
  • High tea is often served in beautiful and elegant settings

Cons:

  • High tea can be expensive, especially at high-end hotels and restaurants
  • It can be difficult to find high tea options outside of major cities
  • High tea can be time-consuming, so it's not ideal for those on a tight schedule

Conclusion

High tea is a quintessentially British tradition that offers a unique and luxurious experience. Whether you choose to book a reservation at a hotel or restaurant or host your own at home, high tea is sure to be a memorable experience. By following the tips and guidelines outlined in this article, you can make the most of your high tea experience and enjoy all the delicious foods and teas that are on offer.
