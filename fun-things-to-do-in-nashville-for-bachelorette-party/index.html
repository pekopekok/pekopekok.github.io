Fun Things To Do In Nashville For Bachelorette Party

If you’re looking for a place to host your bachelorette party with a perfect combination of country music, southern charm, and vibrant nightlife, Nashville is the place to be! Known as the “Music City,” Nashville is the perfect destination for a fun and unforgettable bachelorette party. In this blog, we’ll share some of the best things to do in Nashville for bachelorette party and ensure that you and your squad have a fantastic time!

1. Party on Broadway

No bachelorette party in Nashville is complete without a night out on Broadway. The street is lined with honky-tonks, bars, and restaurants, all with live music and a lively atmosphere. You and your squad can bar hop, dance, and sing along to some of the best country music in the world. Some of the famous honky-tonks on Broadway include Tootsie’s Orchid Lounge, Robert’s Western World, and The Stage.

2. Visit the Country Music Hall of Fame

The Country Music Hall of Fame is located in downtown Nashville and is a must-visit for any music lover. The museum features exhibits on the history of country music, including artifacts, costumes, and instruments from some of the most famous country music stars. You can even take a tour of RCA Studio B, where Elvis Presley and other legendary artists recorded some of their most famous hits.

3. Take a Pedal Tavern Tour

A pedal tavern tour is a fun and unique way to explore the city with your friends. You and your squad can pedal your way through the streets of Nashville on a mobile bar while sipping on your favorite drinks. Some of the popular pedal tavern tours in Nashville include the Sprocket Rocket, Nashville Pedal Tavern, and Honky Tonk Party Express.

4. Visit the Johnny Cash Museum

Located in downtown Nashville, the Johnny Cash Museum is a must-visit for any fan of the Man in Black. The museum features exhibits on the life and music of Johnny Cash, including personal artifacts, costumes, and instruments. You can even see the famous black suit that Cash wore in his music videos.

5. Explore the Gulch

The Gulch is a trendy neighborhood in Nashville with plenty of restaurants, bars, and shops. You and your squad can explore the vibrant street art, grab a bite to eat at one of the many restaurants, or enjoy a drink at a rooftop bar. Some of the popular spots in The Gulch include Milk & Honey, The Pub Nashville, and Biscuit Love.

6. Go Honky Tonkin’

Honky Tonkin’ is a quintessential Nashville experience. You and your squad can put on your cowboy boots and hit up some of the famous honky-tonks in the city. You can dance, drink, and sing along to some of the best country music in the world. Some of the popular honky-tonks in Nashville include Tootsie’s Orchid Lounge, The Stage, and Roberts Western World.

7. Have a Spa Day

After a night out on the town, you and your squad can relax and unwind with a spa day. Nashville has plenty of spas that offer massages, facials, and other treatments to help you feel rejuvenated. Some of the popular spas in Nashville include The Woodhouse Day Spa, Escape Day Spa, and Relache Spa.

8. Visit the Cheekwood Estate & Gardens

If you’re looking for a more laid-back activity, the Cheekwood Estate & Gardens is a beautiful and peaceful place to visit. The estate features a museum of art, beautiful gardens, and walking trails. You and your squad can stroll through the gardens, take a guided tour, or enjoy a picnic on the lawn.

9. Go on a Food Tour

Nashville is known for its delicious southern cuisine, and a food tour is a great way to sample some of the best dishes in the city. You and your squad can take a guided tour of some of the best restaurants in Nashville and try dishes like hot chicken, barbecue, and biscuits. Some of the popular food tour companies in Nashville include Walk Eat Nashville, Music City Bites & Sites, and Nashville Food Tours.

10. Attend a Show at the Grand Ole Opry

The Grand Ole Opry is a legendary venue that has hosted some of the biggest names in country music. You and your squad can attend a show and listen to live performances by current country music stars, as well as classic country music legends. The Grand Ole Opry is a must-visit for any music lover.

Conclusion

Nashville offers endless fun and exciting activities for a bachelorette party. From partying on Broadway to exploring the beautiful gardens at the Cheekwood Estate, there’s something for everyone in Nashville. We hope this guide has given you some inspiration for planning your bachelorette party in Music City. Have a blast!
