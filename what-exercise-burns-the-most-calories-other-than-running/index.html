What Exercise Burns The Most Calories Other Than Running

If you're looking to burn calories, there are plenty of exercises that can help you achieve your goal. While running is a popular choice for many, it's not the only way to get your heart rate up and torch those calories. In this article, we'll explore some of the best exercises for burning calories and how you can incorporate them into your workout routine.

Weightlifting

Weightlifting is a fantastic way to burn calories and build muscle. When you lift weights, your body burns calories not only during the workout, but also after as your muscles repair and grow. According to the American Council on Exercise, weightlifting can burn up to 133 calories in just 10 minutes.

When weightlifting, focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once, such as squats, deadlifts, and bench presses. Aim for 2-3 sets of 8-12 reps per exercise, with a weight that challenges you but still allows you to maintain proper form.

Cycling

Cycling is a low-impact exercise that's great for burning calories and improving cardiovascular health. You can cycle outdoors or indoors on a stationary bike. According to the Harvard Health Blog, cycling at a moderate pace can burn up to 298 calories in just 30 minutes.

If you're cycling outdoors, be sure to wear a helmet and follow traffic laws. If you're using a stationary bike, adjust the resistance to increase the intensity of your workout.

Swimming

Swimming is a full-body workout that can burn a lot of calories. It's also low-impact, making it a great option for those with joint pain or injuries. According to the American Council on Exercise, swimming can burn up to 223 calories in just 30 minutes.

To get the most out of your swim workout, focus on different strokes and intensities. You can try freestyle, breaststroke, backstroke, or butterfly, and vary your pace from a leisurely swim to an all-out sprint.

Jumping Rope

Jumping rope is an excellent cardiovascular exercise that can burn a lot of calories in a short amount of time. It's also a low-cost and portable exercise option that you can do anywhere. According to the American Council on Exercise, jumping rope can burn up to 107 calories in just 10 minutes.

When jumping rope, focus on proper form and start with a slow pace. As you get more comfortable, increase your speed and try different variations, such as single-leg jumps or double unders.

Hiking

Hiking is a great way to burn calories while enjoying the outdoors. It's also an excellent option for those who prefer low-impact exercises. According to the American Hiking Society, hiking can burn up to 400-700 calories per hour, depending on the terrain and intensity.

When hiking, be sure to wear appropriate shoes and clothing, and bring plenty of water and snacks. Start with an easy trail and gradually increase the difficulty as you get more comfortable.

Boxing

Boxing is a high-intensity exercise that can burn a lot of calories while also improving your coordination and agility. You can box with a partner or use a punching bag. According to the American Council on Exercise, boxing can burn up to 400-600 calories per hour.

When boxing, focus on proper form and start with basic punches, such as jabs and crosses. Gradually add in more advanced moves, such as hooks and uppercuts.

Rowing

Rowing is a full-body workout that can burn a lot of calories while also improving your cardiovascular health. You can use a rowing machine at the gym or row outdoors on a lake or river. According to Harvard Health Publishing, rowing at a moderate pace can burn up to 210 calories in just 30 minutes.

When rowing, focus on proper form and start with a slow pace. As you get more comfortable, increase your speed and try different variations, such as rowing with one arm or with a resistance band.

Stair Climbing

Stair climbing is a great way to burn calories while also strengthening your lower body. You can climb stairs indoors or outdoors, such as at a stadium or park. According to the American Council on Exercise, stair climbing can burn up to 180-266 calories in just 20 minutes.

When stair climbing, focus on proper form and start with a slow pace. As you get more comfortable, increase your speed and try different variations, such as skipping a step or taking two steps at a time.

Dancing

Dancing is a fun and high-energy exercise that can burn a lot of calories while also improving your coordination and balance. You can take a dance class or dance at home to your favorite music. According to the American Council on Exercise, dancing can burn up to 221-443 calories in just 30 minutes.

When dancing, focus on having fun and moving to the beat. Try different styles, such as hip hop or salsa, and vary your intensity from a slow dance to a fast-paced routine.

FAQs

What's the best exercise for burning calories?

The best exercise for burning calories is the one that you enjoy and can stick to over the long term. Some great options include weightlifting, cycling, swimming, jumping rope, hiking, boxing, rowing, stair climbing, and dancing.

How many calories can I burn in a workout?

The number of calories you burn in a workout depends on several factors, including your weight, age, gender, and intensity level. On average, you can burn up to 300-600 calories in a 30-minute workout.

How often should I exercise to burn calories?

To burn calories and see results, aim to exercise for at least 30 minutes a day, five days a week. You can also break up your workouts into shorter sessions throughout the day.

What should I eat before and after a workout?

Before a workout, aim to eat a balanced meal that includes carbohydrates and protein, such as a banana with peanut butter or a turkey sandwich. After a workout, focus on replenishing your body with protein and carbohydrates, such as a protein shake or a chicken and rice bowl.

What are the benefits of burning calories through exercise?

Burning calories through exercise can help you achieve and maintain a healthy weight, improve your cardiovascular health, boost your mood and energy levels, and reduce your risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes and heart disease.

Conclusion

While running is a great way to burn calories, it's not the only option. By incorporating a variety of exercises into your workout routine, you can keep things interesting and challenge your body in new ways. Whether you prefer weightlifting, cycling, swimming, jumping rope, hiking, boxing, rowing, stair climbing, or dancing, there's an exercise out there for you that can help you achieve your fitness goals.
