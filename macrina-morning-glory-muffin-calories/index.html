Description of Macrina Morning Glory Muffins

Macrina Morning Glory Muffins are a popular breakfast item that you can find at many coffee shops and bakeries. These muffins are made with a variety of healthy ingredients, such as whole wheat flour, carrots, raisins, and nuts. They're also sweetened with honey instead of sugar, which makes them a healthier option.

Plus, if you pair the muffin with a cup of coffee or tea, you'll have a filling breakfast that will keep you satisfied until lunchtime.

How to Make Macrina Morning Glory Muffins

If you're interested in making your own Macrina Morning Glory Muffins, then you'll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts

Here are the steps to make the muffins:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, brown sugar, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, vegetable oil, applesauce, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  5. Stir in the grated carrots, raisins, and chopped walnuts.
  6. Scoop the batter into a muffin tin lined with paper liners.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  8. Let the muffins cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips for Making the Best Macrina Morning Glory Muffins

If you want to make the best Macrina Morning Glory Muffins possible, then here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use fresh ingredients whenever possible.
  • Don't overmix the batter, as this can make the muffins tough.
  • Use paper liners to make it easier to remove the muffins from the tin.
  • Store leftover muffins in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.
