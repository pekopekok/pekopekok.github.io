Description

Morning Tea Shout is a tradition in Australia and New Zealand. It is a way to socialize and show appreciation to your colleagues, friends, or family by offering them tea, coffee, or some snacks. It usually happens in the workplace, where one person takes responsibility for providing tea and snacks for everyone in the group. The person who is responsible for providing tea and snacks is called the Shouter.

Usually, Morning Tea Shout happens in the mid-morning break, around 10:30 or 11:00 am. It is a great way to take a short break from work and socialize with your colleagues. It is also an opportunity to build relationships and get to know your co-workers on a more personal level.

How to

Doing a Morning Tea Shout is easy. All you need to do is choose a day and time, inform your colleagues, and bring some tea, coffee, or snacks. It is also a good idea to ask your colleagues if they have any preferences or dietary restrictions.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to do a Morning Tea Shout:

  1. Choose a day and time that works for everyone.
  2. Inform your colleagues about the Morning Tea Shout.
  3. Ask your colleagues if they have any preferences or dietary restrictions.
  4. Bring some tea, coffee, or snacks.
  5. Set up a table or a space where everyone can gather.
  6. Enjoy your tea, coffee, or snacks with your colleagues.

Tips

Here are some tips to make your Morning Tea Shout more enjoyable:

  • Choose a variety of snacks to cater to everyone's taste.
  • Ask your colleagues to bring their favorite tea or coffee to share.
  • Use this opportunity to talk to your colleagues about something other than work.

Solution

Morning Tea Shout is a great way to build relationships with your colleagues, show appreciation, and take a short break from work. It is easy to do and does not require much effort. So why not start doing it in your workplace?
