Benefits of Tahajjud Prayer

Tahajjud prayer is a voluntary prayer that is performed after the Isha prayer and before the Fajr prayer. It is a special prayer that is highly recommended by Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and has many benefits, both spiritual and physical.

The spiritual benefits of Tahajjud prayer include getting closer to Allah, seeking forgiveness for sins, and feeling a sense of peace and tranquillity. The physical benefits of Tahajjud prayer include improved sleep patterns, increased energy levels, and a stronger immune system.

How to Perform Tahajjud Prayer

Performing Tahajjud prayer is relatively simple and follows the same format as any other prayer. Here are the steps to perform Tahajjud prayer:

  1. Perform the Isha prayer as normal
  2. Go to sleep for a few hours
  3. Wake up in the middle of the night
  4. Perform 2-12 rak’ahs (units) of prayer
  5. Make dua (supplication) to Allah

It is important to note that Tahajjud prayer is a voluntary prayer and is not mandatory. It is up to the individual to decide how many rak’ahs they want to perform.

What Time Does Tahajjud Start?

The time for Tahajjud prayer starts after the Isha prayer and continues until the Fajr prayer. However, the best time to perform Tahajjud prayer is during the last third of the night, which is the time when Allah descends to the lowest heaven and asks if anyone is seeking forgiveness so that He may forgive them.

The exact time for Tahajjud prayer varies depending on the location and time of year. It is best to check your local prayer timetable or use an app to find the exact time for Tahajjud prayer in your area.

Tips for Performing Tahajjud Prayer

Here are some tips to help you get started with Tahajjud prayer:

  • Start small – begin by performing just two rak’ahs and gradually increase the number over time
  • Set an alarm – wake up in the middle of the night to perform Tahajjud prayer
  • Make dua – use the time after prayer to make dua to Allah and seek forgiveness
  • Be consistent – try to perform Tahajjud prayer regularly, even if it’s just once a week
