How to Have Keg Beer at Home - A Step by Step Guide | 2023

Welcome to our comprehensive guide on how to have keg beer at home! Whether you're a beer enthusiast or just looking for a fun way to enjoy a cold one, having a keg at home is a great option. In this post, we'll walk you through everything you need to know, from buying a keg to tapping it and storing it properly. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Buying a Keg
  • Finding a Tap
  • Tapping the Keg
  • Storing the Keg
  • Tips for Enjoying Keg Beer at Home

Buying a Keg

The first step in having keg beer at home is to buy a keg. Kegs are available in a variety of sizes, from 5-gallon to 15.5-gallon, and can be purchased from many liquor stores, breweries, and distributors. You'll also need to decide on the type of beer you want to serve, as different types of beer require different types of kegs.

When buying a keg, make sure to check the expiration date and ensure that the keg has been handled properly. You don't want to end up with a flat or spoiled keg!

Finding a Tap

Once you have your keg, you'll need to find a tap to dispense the beer. Taps can be purchased or rented from many liquor stores, breweries, and party rental companies. Make sure to choose a tap that is compatible with the type of keg you have.

If you're unsure about how to use a tap, ask the retailer for instructions or watch a tutorial online. It's important to know how to properly tap a keg to avoid any spills or waste.

Tapping the Keg

Now that you have your keg and tap, it's time to tap the keg! Make sure the keg is properly chilled before tapping, as warm beer will produce excess foam. To tap the keg, follow these steps:

  1. Attach the tap to the keg by inserting the tap into the hole in the top of the keg.
  2. Turn the tap to the "on" position.
  3. Pull the handle forward to start the flow of beer.
  4. Adjust the pressure as needed to control the flow of beer.

Storing the Keg

After tapping the keg, it's important to store it properly to ensure the beer stays fresh. Keep the keg cool at all times, ideally between 34°F and 38°F. If the keg gets too warm, the beer will spoil and produce off-flavors.

You can store the keg in a refrigerator or a kegerator, which is a specialized refrigerator designed for kegs. If you don't have a kegerator, you can use a large bucket filled with ice to keep the keg cool.

Tips for Enjoying Keg Beer at Home

  • Invite friends over to enjoy the keg with you. Kegs are great for parties and gatherings.
  • Experiment with different types of beer to find your favorite.
  • Use a beer glass to enhance the flavor and aroma of the beer.
  • Don't forget to clean the tap and keg after use to prevent bacteria buildup.

FAQ

How long will a keg last?

A keg will last for about 2-3 months before the beer starts to spoil. Once you tap the keg, the beer will last for about 2-3 days before it starts to lose its carbonation.

Do I need a special refrigerator for a keg?

No, you don't need a special refrigerator for a keg. However, a kegerator is a convenient option as it is designed specifically for kegs and has a tap built in.

Can I return a keg?

Some retailers allow keg returns, while others do not. Check with the retailer before purchasing a keg to find out their return policy.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Convenient and fun way to enjoy beer at home
  • Great for parties and gatherings
  • Allows you to try different types of beer
  • Cost-effective if you drink a lot of beer

Cons:

  • Requires storage space and a tap
  • Can be expensive upfront
  • Beer may spoil if not stored properly
  • Requires cleaning and maintenance

Overall, having keg beer at home can be a great option for beer lovers and party hosts alike. Just make sure to follow our guide and store the keg properly to ensure the beer stays fresh and tasty. Cheers!
