What To Do On Memorial Day In New York A Complete Guide

If you're looking for things to do on Memorial Day in New York, you're in luck. The city that never sleeps has a plethora of options for everyone. From parades to concerts, there's something for everyone to enjoy. In this guide, we'll take you through what to do on Memorial Day in New York.

Parades

One of the most popular things to do on Memorial Day in New York is to attend a parade. The largest and most famous parade is the National Memorial Day Parade in Washington, D.C. However, New York has its own parade that is just as impressive. The Little Neck-Douglaston Memorial Day Parade is the largest in the city and attracts thousands of spectators each year.

Concerts

For music lovers, there are plenty of concerts to attend on Memorial Day in New York. The Governors Ball Music Festival is one of the biggest events of the year and takes place on Randall's Island. The festival features a diverse lineup of music acts, including rock, pop, and hip-hop. In addition to the Governors Ball, there are many other concerts and music events happening throughout the city.

Outdoor Activities

Memorial Day is the unofficial start of summer, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in New York. Central Park is a great place to start, with its many walking trails, picnic areas, and sports fields. The Brooklyn Bridge Park is another popular spot, with its stunning views of the Manhattan skyline.

Museums

If you're looking for something indoors to do on Memorial Day in New York, there are plenty of museums to explore. The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) is one of the most famous art museums in the world, featuring works by Picasso, Van Gogh, and other famous artists. The American Museum of Natural History is another popular choice, with its impressive collection of dinosaur fossils, animal specimens, and other natural wonders.

Memorials

Memorial Day is a day to remember and honor those who have served our country. There are many memorials and monuments throughout New York that are worth visiting, including the 9/11 Memorial and Museum, the Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum, and the USS Intrepid.

Food

New York is known for its food, and Memorial Day is a great time to indulge in some delicious eats. Many restaurants and food vendors offer Memorial Day specials, including BBQ, seafood, and other summer favorites. You can also check out one of the many food festivals happening throughout the city.

Shopping

For those who love to shop, Memorial Day weekend is the perfect time to score some deals. Many stores and shopping centers offer Memorial Day sales, with discounts on clothing, electronics, and more. The Woodbury Common Premium Outlets is a popular destination for bargain hunters, with over 250 stores offering discounts up to 65% off.

Tips for Enjoying Memorial Day in New York

Here are a few tips to help you make the most of your Memorial Day in New York:
  • Dress comfortably and wear sunscreen if you plan to spend time outdoors.
  • Plan ahead and book tickets for events and attractions in advance.
  • Avoid driving in the city, as traffic can be heavy. Instead, take public transportation or walk.
  • Bring cash, as some vendors and events may not accept credit cards.
  • Stay hydrated, especially if you'll be spending time outside in the sun.

FAQs

What is Memorial Day?

Memorial Day is a federal holiday in the United States that honors and remembers those who have died while serving in the military.

When is Memorial Day?

Memorial Day is observed on the last Monday in May.

What is there to do on Memorial Day in New York?

There are many things to do on Memorial Day in New York, including attending parades, concerts, and festivals, exploring museums and memorials, enjoying outdoor activities, shopping, and eating delicious food.

Is Memorial Day a busy time in New York?

Yes, Memorial Day weekend is a popular time for tourists to visit New York, so expect crowds and long lines at popular attractions and events. It's best to plan ahead and book tickets and reservations in advance.
