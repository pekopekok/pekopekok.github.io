What Is A Good Eating Schedule For A 12 Year Old

Written by Alvine May 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

A 12 year old should have three meals per day and two healthy snacks .

Description

As a parent, it can be challenging to determine what a good eating schedule is for your 12-year-old child. With so many conflicting opinions and fad diets out there, it can be tough to know what is best for your child's health. In this article, we will discuss what a good eating schedule for a 12-year-old looks like and provide tips on how to implement it.

How to

Step 1: Breakfast

Breakfast is the most important meal of the day, especially for growing children. A good breakfast should consist of a balance of protein, healthy fats, and carbohydrates. Some examples include oatmeal with fruit and nuts, scrambled eggs with whole-grain toast, or a smoothie with Greek yogurt and berries.

Step 2: Snacks

Snacks are important for keeping your child's energy levels up throughout the day. Aim for two healthy snacks per day, such as apple slices with almond butter, veggies and hummus, or a small yogurt with granola.

Step 3: Lunch

Lunch should be a well-balanced meal that includes protein, healthy fats, and carbohydrates. Some examples include grilled chicken with vegetables and brown rice, a turkey and avocado sandwich on whole-grain bread, or a quinoa salad with veggies and feta cheese.

Step 4: Dinner

Dinner should also be a well-balanced meal that includes protein, healthy fats, and carbohydrates. Some examples include baked salmon with roasted vegetables and sweet potatoes, whole-grain pasta with tomato sauce and turkey meatballs, or a stir-fry with tofu, veggies, and brown rice.

Step 5: Hydration

Hydration is essential for good health, especially for growing children. Encourage your child to drink water throughout the day and limit sugary drinks like soda and juice.

Step 6: Consistency

Consistency is key when it comes to a good eating schedule. Try to stick to a regular meal and snack schedule to help your child's body regulate hunger and energy levels.

Step 7: Limit Processed Foods

Processed foods can be high in sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats, which can be detrimental to your child's health. Limit processed foods as much as possible and opt for whole, nutrient-dense foods instead.

Step 8: Involve Your Child

Involving your child in meal planning and preparation can help them develop healthy eating habits and a positive relationship with food. Let them choose healthy snacks and help them prepare meals.

Step 9: Be a Good Role Model

Children learn by example, so it's important to be a good role model when it comes to healthy eating habits. Make sure to eat a variety of healthy foods yourself and avoid negative comments about food or body image.

Step 10: FAQ

  • How many meals should a 12-year-old have per day?

    • A 12-year-old should have three meals per day and two healthy snacks.

  • What should a healthy snack for a 12-year-old include?

    • A healthy snack for a 12-year-old should include a balance of protein, healthy fats, and carbohydrates. Examples include apple slices with almond butter, veggies and hummus, or a small yogurt with granola.

  • Is it okay for a 12-year-old to skip meals?

    • No, it's not recommended for a 12-year-old to skip meals. Skipping meals can lead to low energy levels, poor concentration, and unhealthy eating habits.

Tips

  • Focus on nutrient-dense foods like fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  • Avoid sugary drinks and limit processed foods as much as possible.
  • Involve your child in meal planning and preparation to help them develop healthy eating habits.
  • Be a good role model by eating a variety of healthy foods yourself and avoiding negative comments about food or body image.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • A good eating schedule can help your child maintain a healthy weight and energy levels.
  • Eating a variety of nutrient-dense foods can provide essential vitamins and minerals for growth and development.
  • Involving your child in meal planning and preparation can help them develop healthy eating habits and a positive relationship with food.

Cons:

  • It can be challenging to implement a good eating schedule, especially if your child is a picky eater.
  • Busy schedules and conflicting priorities can make it difficult to stick to a regular meal and snack schedule.
  • Some healthy foods can be expensive or difficult to find, depending on where you live.
