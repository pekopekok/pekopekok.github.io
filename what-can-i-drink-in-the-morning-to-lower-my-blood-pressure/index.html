What Can I Drink In The Morning To Lower My Blood Pressure

Written by Bobby Apr 27, 2023 · 4 min read

.

If you're struggling with high blood pressure, you're not alone. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), about 45% of adults in the United States have hypertension. Fortunately, there are many ways to lower your blood pressure, including making simple changes to your diet. In this article, we'll explore what you can drink in the morning to lower your blood pressure.

Coffee

Many people rely on a cup of coffee to jump-start their day, but did you know that coffee may also lower your blood pressure? According to a study published in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, drinking coffee can reduce systolic blood pressure by up to 8 mmHg. However, it's important to note that this effect is temporary and may not be consistent across all individuals.

Green Tea

Green tea is known for its many health benefits, including its ability to lower blood pressure. The catechins in green tea have been shown to reduce systolic blood pressure by up to 2.6 mmHg. Additionally, green tea contains antioxidants that can protect against cardiovascular disease.

Hibiscus Tea

Hibiscus tea is a popular beverage in many cultures, and for good reason. Studies have shown that hibiscus tea can lower both systolic and diastolic blood pressure. One study found that drinking hibiscus tea daily for six weeks reduced systolic blood pressure by an average of 11.2 mmHg.

Beet Juice

Beet juice is a great source of nitrates, which can help lower blood pressure. When you consume nitrates, your body converts them into nitric oxide, which relaxes and widens your blood vessels. This allows blood to flow more easily and can lower your blood pressure. One study found that drinking 250 ml of beet juice daily for four weeks reduced systolic blood pressure by an average of 8 mmHg.

Pomegranate Juice

Pomegranate juice is rich in antioxidants, which can help reduce inflammation and protect against cardiovascular disease. Studies have also shown that drinking pomegranate juice can lower both systolic and diastolic blood pressure. One study found that drinking 240 ml of pomegranate juice daily for four weeks reduced systolic blood pressure by an average of 5 mmHg.

Water

Drinking enough water is important for maintaining healthy blood pressure. When you're dehydrated, your blood vessels constrict, which can cause your blood pressure to rise. Aim to drink at least eight glasses of water per day to stay hydrated and keep your blood pressure in check.

Milk

Milk is a good source of calcium and vitamin D, which are important for maintaining healthy blood pressure. Studies have shown that people who consume more dairy products have lower blood pressure than those who consume less. However, it's important to choose low-fat or fat-free milk to avoid consuming too much saturated fat.

Orange Juice

Orange juice is rich in vitamin C, which can help lower blood pressure. Studies have shown that consuming vitamin C can reduce both systolic and diastolic blood pressure. Aim to drink freshly squeezed orange juice rather than store-bought juice, which is often high in added sugar.

Alcohol

While small amounts of alcohol may have a beneficial effect on blood pressure, excessive alcohol consumption can be harmful. Drinking more than one or two drinks per day can raise your blood pressure and increase your risk of cardiovascular disease. If you choose to drink alcohol, do so in moderation.

Conclusion

In conclusion, there are many drinks you can consume in the morning to lower your blood pressure. Coffee, green tea, hibiscus tea, beet juice, pomegranate juice, water, milk, and orange juice all have been shown to have blood pressure-lowering effects. However, it's important to remember that these effects may not be consistent across all individuals and that moderation is key. Talk to your doctor about the best ways to lower your blood pressure and maintain a healthy lifestyle.
