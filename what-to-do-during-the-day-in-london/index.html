What To Do During The Day In London A Relaxing Guide

Whether you are a local or a tourist, London is a city that never sleeps. With so many things to do and see, it can be overwhelming to plan a day out. In this guide, we will provide you with some ideas on what to do during the day in London, including popular attractions, hidden gems, and relaxing activities. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents:

  • Popular Attractions
  • Hidden Gems
  • Relaxing Activities

Popular Attractions

If you are visiting London for the first time, or you want to see some of the city's most iconic landmarks, these popular attractions are a must-visit:

The Tower of London

The Tower of London is a historic castle located in central London. It was built in the 11th century and has served many purposes over the years, including as a palace, a prison, and a treasury. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction where visitors can see the Crown Jewels, the White Tower, and the famous Beefeaters.

The British Museum

The British Museum is one of the world's oldest and largest museums. It was founded in 1753 and has over 8 million objects on display, including the Rosetta Stone, the Parthenon sculptures, and the mummies of Ancient Egypt. The museum is free to enter, but some exhibitions may require a ticket.

The London Eye

The London Eye is a giant Ferris wheel located on the South Bank of the River Thames. It was built in 1999 and offers stunning views of the city from its 32 capsules. A ride on the London Eye takes approximately 30 minutes and is a popular activity for tourists and locals alike.

Hidden Gems

If you want to explore some of London's lesser-known attractions, these hidden gems are worth a visit:

The Hampstead Pergola and Hill Garden

The Hampstead Pergola and Hill Garden is a hidden oasis located in the heart of Hampstead. It was built in the early 20th century and features a stunning pergola covered in climbing plants, as well as a beautiful hill garden. The pergola and garden are free to enter and offer a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

The Leighton House Museum

The Leighton House Museum is a beautiful Victorian mansion located in Holland Park. It was the former home of the artist Frederic Leighton and is now a museum dedicated to his life and work. The museum features a stunning Arab Hall, as well as many of Leighton's paintings and sculptures.

The Sky Garden

The Sky Garden is a public garden located on the 35th floor of the Walkie Talkie building in the City of London. It offers panoramic views of the city and is free to enter, although tickets must be booked in advance. The Sky Garden also features a restaurant and bar, making it a great spot for a relaxing lunch or evening drink.

Relaxing Activities

If you want to take a break from sightseeing and do something relaxing, these activities are perfect:

A Walk in Hyde Park

Hyde Park is a beautiful park located in central London. It covers over 350 acres and features many walking trails, lakes, and gardens. A walk in Hyde Park is a great way to escape the city and enjoy some fresh air.

A Spa Day

London has many spas and wellness centers where you can relax and unwind. Some popular options include the Cowshed Spa in Shoreditch, the Aman Spa in Mayfair, and the ESPA Life at the Corinthia Hotel. Treatments range from massages and facials to hydrotherapy and yoga.

A River Cruise

A river cruise is a relaxing way to see the sights of London from a different perspective. There are many companies that offer river cruises, including City Cruises, Thames Clippers, and Bateaux London. Choose from a sightseeing tour, a dinner cruise, or a jazz cruise.

Conclusion

London is a city that has something for everyone. Whether you want to see the popular attractions, explore hidden gems, or relax and unwind, there are plenty of options to choose from. We hope this guide has given you some ideas on what to do during the day in London. Enjoy your trip!
