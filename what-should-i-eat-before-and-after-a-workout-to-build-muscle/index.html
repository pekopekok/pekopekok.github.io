What Should I Eat Before And After A Workout To Build Muscle

When it comes to building muscle, a proper diet is just as important as a consistent workout routine. In fact, what you eat before and after a workout can have a significant impact on your muscle-building progress. In this article, we'll go over what you should eat before and after a workout to build muscle, as well as some tips and tricks to help you maximize your gains.

  2. What to Eat After a Workout

What to Eat Before a Workout

Before a workout, it's important to fuel your body with the right nutrients to help you power through your routine. Here are some foods you should consider eating before your next gym session:

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are an important source of energy, so it's a good idea to eat some carbs before a workout. However, not all carbs are created equal. Complex carbs like oatmeal, brown rice, and sweet potatoes are digested slowly, providing a steady source of energy throughout your workout. On the other hand, simple carbs like candy and white bread can cause a spike in blood sugar followed by a crash, leaving you feeling sluggish mid-workout.

Protein

Protein is essential for building muscle, so it's a good idea to eat some before a workout. You don't need to go overboard, though. Aim for around 20-30 grams of protein before your workout. Good sources of pre-workout protein include eggs, Greek yogurt, and protein shakes.

Fats

While you don't want to eat a ton of fat before a workout, a little bit can help keep you feeling full and satisfied. Good sources of healthy fats include nuts, avocado, and olive oil.

What to Eat After a Workout

After a workout, your body needs protein and carbs to help repair and rebuild muscle tissue. Here are some foods you should consider eating after your next gym session:

Protein

As we mentioned earlier, protein is essential for building muscle. Aim to consume around 20-30 grams of protein after your workout. Good sources of post-workout protein include chicken, fish, and protein shakes.

Carbohydrates

Similar to before a workout, carbohydrates are important for replenishing energy stores after a workout. Again, aim for complex carbs like quinoa, brown rice, and whole grain bread.

Tips for Building Muscle

Here are some additional tips to help you build muscle:

  • Be consistent with your workouts
  • Progressively increase weight and intensity
  • Get plenty of rest and recovery time
  • Stay hydrated
  • Eat a well-balanced diet with plenty of protein

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I workout first thing in the morning?

If you workout first thing in the morning, it may not be feasible to eat a full meal beforehand. In this case, try eating a small snack that's high in carbs and low in fat and protein, such as a banana or a piece of toast with jam.

Should I eat before or after my workout?

Both! Eating before a workout will give you the energy you need to power through your routine, while eating after a workout will help repair and rebuild muscle tissue.

Conclusion

What you eat before and after a workout can have a significant impact on your muscle-building progress. Aim to eat a combination of protein and carbs before and after your workouts, and don't forget to stay hydrated and get plenty of rest. By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to building the muscle you've always wanted.
