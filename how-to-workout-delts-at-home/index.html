If you're looking to build strong, defined shoulders, then you need to focus on working out your delts. While many people assume that you need to hit the gym to really target this area, it's actually possible to get a great delt workout from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll give you everything you need to know about how to workout delts at home, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and more.

How to Workout Delts at Home

The first step to working out your delts at home is to make sure you have the right equipment. While you can certainly do many exercises with just your bodyweight, having a set of dumbbells or resistance bands will give you more options and help you to progressively overload your muscles.

Once you have your equipment, there are a few key exercises you'll want to focus on to target your delts:

  • Shoulder press
  • Front raises
  • Side raises
  • Rear delt flyes

By incorporating these exercises into your routine, you'll be able to build a strong, balanced set of shoulders that will help you in all areas of your fitness journey.

Step-by-Step Instructions

To perform a shoulder press:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height.
  2. Press the dumbbells overhead, fully extending your arms.
  3. Lower the dumbbells back down to shoulder height.
  4. Repeat for 8-12 reps.

To perform front raises:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand in front of your thighs.
  2. Keeping your arms straight, lift the dumbbells up in front of you until they reach shoulder height.
  3. Lower the dumbbells back down to your thighs.
  4. Repeat for 8-12 reps.

To perform side raises:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides.
  2. Lift the dumbbells out to your sides, keeping your arms straight, until they reach shoulder height.
  3. Lower the dumbbells back down to your sides.
  4. Repeat for 8-12 reps.

To perform rear delt flyes:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides.
  2. Bend forward at the waist, keeping your back flat, until your torso is almost parallel to the ground.
  3. Lift the dumbbells out to your sides, squeezing your shoulder blades together, until they reach shoulder height.
  4. Lower the dumbbells back down to your sides.
  5. Repeat for 8-12 reps.

Tips for a Better Delt Workout

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you work out your delts at home:

  • Start with lighter weights and focus on perfect form before increasing the weight.
  • Don't neglect your rear delts - they're just as important as your front and side delts.
  • Take breaks as needed and don't push yourself too hard too quickly.
  • Consider incorporating resistance bands into your routine for added variety.

The Best Solution for Building Strong Delts

While working out your delts at home can certainly be effective, there's no denying that having access to a gym and a wider range of equipment can be beneficial. If you're serious about building strong, defined shoulders, then consider investing in a gym membership or working with a personal trainer who can give you more targeted guidance.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about working out your delts at home:

What if I don't have any equipment?

You can still get a great delt workout using just your bodyweight. Push-ups, handstand push-ups, and pike push-ups are all effective exercises that can help you build strength in your shoulders.

How often should I work out my delts?

As with any muscle group, you'll want to give your delts time to rest and recover between workouts. Aim to work them out 1-2 times per week.

What if I experience pain or discomfort while working out my delts?

If you experience any pain or discomfort while working out your delts, stop immediately and consult with a medical professional. It's better to be safe than sorry when it comes to your health.

Pros and Cons of Working Out Delts at Home

Here are some pros and cons to consider when it comes to working out your delts at home:

Pros:

  • You can work out on your own schedule without having to go to the gym.
  • You don't need a lot of equipment to get started.
  • You can save money on gym memberships and personal trainers.

Cons:

  • You may have limited equipment options, which can make it difficult to progress over time.
  • You may not have access to a spotter, which can increase your risk of injury.
  • You may not receive the same level of guidance and support as you would in a gym environment.

Ultimately, whether or not you decide to work out your delts at home will depend on your individual preferences and goals. With the right equipment and guidance, however, it's definitely possible to build strong, defined shoulders without ever leaving your house.
