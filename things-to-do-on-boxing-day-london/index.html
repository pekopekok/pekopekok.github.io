Things to Do on Boxing Day London - Relaxing Activities 2023

Welcome to London on Boxing Day! In this article, we will share some relaxing activities for you to enjoy on this special day. Whether you want to explore the city or just take it easy, we've got you covered. So, let's dive in and discover some exciting things to do on Boxing Day London.

Table of Contents

  • Sightseeing
  • Shopping
  • Museums
  • Theatre
  • Parks

Sightseeing

Boxing Day is a perfect day to explore London's famous landmarks and tourist attractions. You can take a walking tour or a hop-on-hop-off bus tour to get a glimpse of the city's history and culture. Some popular landmarks you can visit include:

  • Big Ben and the Houses of Parliament
  • The London Eye
  • Buckingham Palace
  • The Tower of London

With fewer crowds on Boxing Day, you can enjoy the sights without the hustle and bustle of tourists. Don't forget to take some memorable pictures!

Shopping

If you love shopping, then Boxing Day is the day for you! London's famous shopping areas, such as Oxford Street, Regent Street, and Bond Street, offer fantastic sales on this day. You can shop for clothes, electronics, and souvenirs at discounted prices. But be prepared for crowds, as many people flock to these areas for the sales.

Museums

If you're interested in history and culture, then London's museums are a must-visit on Boxing Day. Many museums, such as the British Museum and the National Gallery, offer free entry on this day. You can explore the exhibits and learn about the city's rich history and art. It's a great way to spend a relaxing day with family and friends.

Theatre

London's West End is famous for its theatre productions, and Boxing Day is no exception. You can watch some of the best shows in the world, including musicals, plays, and operas. Many theatres offer discounted tickets on this day, so you can enjoy world-class entertainment without breaking the bank.

Parks

If you want to escape the city's hustle and bustle, then London's parks are a great option. You can enjoy a relaxing walk or a picnic in one of the city's beautiful parks, such as Hyde Park, Regents Park, or St. James's Park. You can also rent a bike or a boat and explore the parks' scenic routes. It's a great way to spend a peaceful day with nature.

Tips for a Relaxing Boxing Day in London

  • Plan ahead and book tickets for popular attractions in advance to avoid long queues.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothes for walking and exploring the city.
  • Carry a bottle of water and some snacks to keep you hydrated and energized throughout the day.
  • Download a map or use a GPS device to navigate the city's streets and attractions.
  • Check the weather forecast and carry an umbrella or a raincoat if necessary.

FAQs

What is Boxing Day?

Boxing Day is a holiday celebrated in many countries, including the UK, Canada, and Australia. It falls on the day after Christmas Day and is traditionally a day for giving gifts to the less fortunate.

Is everything closed on Boxing Day in London?

No, many shops, restaurants, and attractions are open on Boxing Day in London. However, some may have reduced hours or limited services, so it's best to check before visiting.

What is the weather like in London on Boxing Day?

The weather in London on Boxing Day can vary, but it's usually cold and damp. It's best to wear warm clothes and bring an umbrella or a raincoat in case of rain.

Pros and Cons of Spending Boxing Day in London

Pros:

  • Many attractions and shops are open on this day, offering fantastic discounts and deals.
  • There are fewer crowds than during peak tourist season, making it easier to explore the city's sights and attractions.
  • You can enjoy a relaxing day with family and friends, exploring the city's parks and museums or watching a theatre show.

Cons:

  • The weather can be cold and damp, making it uncomfortable for outdoor activities.
  • Some attractions may have reduced hours or limited services on Boxing Day.
  • The city's streets and attractions can be crowded, especially in popular areas such as Oxford Street and Regent Street.

Overall, spending Boxing Day in London can be a fun and relaxing experience, with plenty of activities to suit everyone's interests. Whether you want to explore the city's landmarks, shop for bargains, or enjoy a peaceful day in the park, London has something to offer. So, plan ahead, dress warm, and enjoy the day!
