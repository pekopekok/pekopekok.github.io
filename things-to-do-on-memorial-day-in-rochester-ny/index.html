Things To Do On Memorial Day In Rochester Ny

Written by Jordan Jul 05, 2023 · 4 min read

.

Description:

Memorial Day is a federal holiday in the United States that honors the men and women who died while serving in the U.S. military. It is observed on the last Monday of May each year. Rochester, NY is a great place to spend the holiday with family and friends. There are many activities and events to attend that celebrate the holiday and the brave men and women who fought for our freedom.

Parade and Ceremony

One of the most popular Memorial Day events in Rochester is the parade and ceremony. The parade usually starts at 10 am and runs through downtown Rochester. It features marching bands, military units, and veterans’ groups. The parade ends at the Soldiers and Sailors Monument in Washington Square Park, where a ceremony is held to honor the fallen soldiers. The ceremony includes a wreath-laying ceremony, speeches, and music. It is a great way to pay tribute to those who have served our country.

Picnic in the Park

Memorial Day is a great time to enjoy the outdoors with family and friends. Rochester has many parks that are perfect for a picnic. Some of the popular parks include Highland Park, Genesee Valley Park, and Ontario Beach Park. You can pack a picnic lunch or purchase food from a food truck or concession stand. Many parks also have playgrounds, sports fields, and walking trails to enjoy.

Visit a Museum

Rochester has several museums that are open on Memorial Day. The Rochester Museum and Science Center, the George Eastman Museum, and the Strong National Museum of Play are all great options. You can learn about the history of Rochester, explore the world of photography, or play with interactive exhibits. Many museums also offer special events and activities on Memorial Day.

Attend a Concert

If you enjoy live music, Rochester has several concerts on Memorial Day. The Rochester Philharmonic Orchestra usually performs a Memorial Day concert at Highland Park. The concert is free and features patriotic music and guest performers. The Rochester Lilac Festival also hosts several concerts throughout the day. You can enjoy music while taking in the beautiful scenery.

Watch a Movie

If you prefer to relax indoors, you can watch a movie on Memorial Day. Many movie theaters in Rochester have special showings of patriotic films. You can also watch a movie at home on Netflix or another streaming service. Some popular patriotic films include Saving Private Ryan, Black Hawk Down, and American Sniper.

Take a Walk

Rochester has many walking trails that are perfect for a leisurely stroll on Memorial Day. Some popular options include the Genesee Riverway Trail, the Erie Canalway Trail, and the Rochester Greenway Trail. You can enjoy the scenery, get some exercise, and spend time with loved ones.

Go Fishing

If you enjoy fishing, Memorial Day is a great time to cast your line. Rochester has many lakes and rivers that are perfect for fishing. Some popular fishing spots include Lake Ontario, Irondequoit Bay, and the Genesee River. You can catch trout, salmon, bass, and more. Don’t forget to get a fishing license before you go.

Visit a Memorial

Memorial Day is a time to reflect on the sacrifices of the men and women who have served our country. Rochester has several memorials that honor our fallen soldiers. The Vietnam Veterans Memorial, the Korean War Memorial, and the World War II Memorial are all located in Highland Park. You can pay your respects and remember those who have served.

Volunteer

Another way to honor the men and women who have served our country is to volunteer. Many organizations in Rochester need volunteers on Memorial Day. You can volunteer at a local VA hospital, participate in a charity run, or help out at a community event. Volunteering is a great way to give back and show your appreciation.

Conclusion

Memorial Day is a time to remember and honor those who have served our country. Rochester, NY offers many activities and events to celebrate the holiday. Whether you attend a parade, have a picnic in the park, or volunteer, you can spend the day with family and friends while paying tribute to our fallen soldiers.
